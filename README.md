# Worldlayoffs-Data-Analysis


Questions
Do high funding actually reduce the risk of failures?
Are only a few companies driving majority of the layoffs or is the phenemenon evenly distributed across companies?
Why are some industries more vulnerable to economic stress while some show resilience?
Is it the developed or the developing countries which were more impacted by layoffs?



Findings
Companies like Britishvolt and Quibi raised more than a billion dollars and still laid-off 100% of their employees.
Big companies like Amazon, Google and Meta alone contribute to ~10.5% of the total layoffs.
Demand-driven sectors like Consumers and Retail alone contribute to ~23% of total layoffs.
Only 3 out of the top 10 countries in total layoffs are developing countries with USA and India being on the top 2.




Insights
Several companies with high funding still experienced complete shutdowns indicating that access to capital does not necessitate business survival.
A few companies are driving most of the layoffs whcih point towards internal restructuring or financial stress rather than a market-wide trend.
Certain industries like consumer and retail are more vulnerable to layoffs as they are demand-sensitive, highly competitive, operate on low profit-margins, etc.
Developed countries are most affected by layoffs given certain conditions like strong labour laws making labour costly, high automation, frequent corporate restructing, more exposure to global markets, etc.
